About

Dr. Max Blumberg is an occupational and environmental medicine specialist who diagnoses and treats work‑related and environmentally induced injuries and illnesses. He cares for patients after traumatic accidents and for those exposed to toxic metals, chemicals, infectious agents, and other hazards. He also has expertise in the evaluation and management of repetitive strain injuries and performs independent medical evaluations (IMEs).

  • Certified California Qualified Medical Examiner (QME)
  • MD, University at Buffalo Jacobs School of Medicine and Biological Sciences (SUNY)
  • Residency in Occupational & Environmental Medicine, UCSF (chief resident)
  • MPH, University of California, Berkeley

Services

  • Independent medical evaluations (QME)
  • Medical‑legal reports and expert opinions
  • Clinical care for occupational and environmental exposures

Experience

As Associate Medical Director of UCSF Occupational Health Services, Dr. Blumberg advocates for the health and safety of health care workers and leads efforts to prevent workplace injury and illness. Prior to UCSF, he served as a staff physician for the Erie County Department of Health in Buffalo, NY.
